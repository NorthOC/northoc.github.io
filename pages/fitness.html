    Fitness

    I'm not motivated, nor am I passionate about fitness - I suck it up and do it for simple reasons: to look hot and feel good.

    So, here's some advice on how to get fit. And a backstory to go along with that.

    Prologue: No life

    In my teens I was a 360 noscope gamer (cringe). This alone should be enough for you to understand on what kind of microwaved goodies and various soysnacks I used to live on day by day.

    Breaking-point: The weight-in

    They don't call it sweet sixteen for nothing.

    It was time for the annual medical check-up that we needed for school. After the doctor told me to step on a scale, as she jotted down the numbers 7 and 0, she said to me that I now was classed "overweight" in medical terms.

    ME? OVERWEIGHT?! There has to be some sort of mistake!

    But guess what - it was not a mistake. I was at 70kg and honestly shocked. To be a fat kid was something I feared, and that fear sparked and urgency in me to start doing something about it.

    Cardio is a scam

    I called one of my gaming buddies on Skype and we decided to both start running everyday after school at the same time and then call each other (we lived in different cities) to talk about it.

    My friend stopped after a month but, four years down the line, I was still busting out that 4-7km a day, five times a week, religiously. And guess what? THE RESULTS WERE SHIT!

    I looked like a stickman with some lower belly fat and not a single visible muscle.

    Forming the foundation

    After finishing studies abroad, I decided to go to the military to clear my head and figure out what to do next.

    To prepare my body for what was to come, I started doing calisthetics 5 times a week. Honestly, it was a poor attempt due to a lack of knowledge but I did manage to gain a bit of muscle before the beginning of service.

    One day in the military, when doing intense training, I busted my knee and had to go through various doctors for three weeks. The wear and tear of years of running on asphalt has finally caught up with me.

    I made a decision to stop running to avoid any further damage.

    Building the base

    Due to the 9 months in the military, I had gained some muscle, so, to maintain that, I started doing calisthetics again.

    The Covid-19 pandemic was good due to being able to work out at home! I gained a lot of upper body muscle. My PR's on max pull ups were rising weekly.

    To fully get good body proportions, I started doing ab and legs workouts.

    Epilogue: I built my temple

    Sometimes I look in the mirror and think that I look like Tyler Durden from that basement scene. Maybe it is my imagination, but it has definitely had a deep positive impact on the way I carry myself and how others react to me, especially women.

    The best part is that I'm now keeping it up between 65-70kg, except this time it is mostly muscle.

    I want to get fit too! Where do I start?

    Great, here's everything. As for how to do each exercise, look it up on YouTube.

    All you need is a yoga mat and a pull-up bar and you're good to go.

    Schedule:

    • Monday: Upper body.
    • Tuesday: Upper body.
    • Wednesday: Abs + Legs.
    • Thursday: Upper body.
    • Friday: Abs.

    Do A warmup before a workout!

    Upper body

    In such order: Pull ups -> Triceps dips -> Push ups -> Chin ups

    Do each exercise 5 times, each time as many reps as possible and rest for 1 minute between each attempt. After the fifth time, rest for 2 minutes before moving onto the next exercise.

    So, in detail, do pull ups once for a max number of reps, then chill for a minute, do pull ups again, chill for a minute... do pull ups for the fifth time, chill for two minutes and move onto the next excercise.

    Abs

    10 different abs exercises. For each exercise, you have to either do 50 reps (ex: 50 reps of russian twists) or do a hold for a minute (ex: hold a plank for one minute). Rest when needed.

    For inspiration on what exercises you can do, look up Fraser Wilson. If you choose to do harder exercises (ex. leg lifts), you will have faster and better results.

    Legs

    In such order: Lunges -> Leg raises -> Quadruped single leg lift -> Standing calf raises -> Wall-sit -> Squats -> Plank

    For each exercise, you have to either do 50 reps (ex: 50 reps of lunges) or do a hold for a minute (ex: hold a wall-sit for one minute). Between each exercise, do 70 reps of jump rope skips. Rest when needed.
